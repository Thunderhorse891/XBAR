# XBAR Records: Customer Interview Pack

## Purpose

This pack is for early discovery conversations with breeders, ranch operators, sales-focused horse businesses, and records-heavy equine operations.

The goal is not to pitch the app.

The goal is to learn:

- where paperwork and record chaos actually hurts
- how sales get slowed down
- whether ownership and transfer tracking is painful enough to pay for
- whether OCR-assisted intake is compelling
- what price range and buying motion feel realistic

## Interview Goal

By the end of 5 to 10 interviews, we should know:

- which customer segment is the best first buyer
- which pain is strongest
- what existing tools they are unhappy with
- what “must-have” workflow XBAR should lead with
- whether the wedge is strong enough to charge meaningful money

## Best Interview Targets

Prioritize people who match this profile:

- 20 to 200 horses
- recurring breeding or sale activity
- heavy AQHA or registration paperwork
- multiple owners, partners, or staff stakeholders
- enough operational value that bad records are expensive

Good interview candidates:

- ranch owners
- ranch managers
- operations/admin leads
- sale-focused breeders
- performance horse businesses

Lower-priority interview candidates:

- hobby owners
- boarding barns with low paperwork complexity
- people mainly needing feeding or schedule tools

## Outreach Message

Use this as a DM, text, or email:

Hi [Name], I am working on XBAR Records, a product for horse records, ownership paperwork, document intake, and sale readiness. I am not trying to sell you anything right now. I am trying to understand how real horse businesses handle records, transfers, and sale packets so I do not build the wrong thing. Would you be open to a 20-minute conversation this week or next?

## Call Opening Script

Thanks for taking the time. I am building XBAR Records, and I want to understand how you actually manage horse records, ownership paperwork, and sale prep today. I am not here to pitch you. I am trying to learn where the mess and friction really are so I can build something worth paying for.

## Core Interview Questions

1. How do you currently manage horse records, ownership paperwork, medical documents, and sale packets?
2. What tools are you using today: spreadsheets, cloud folders, email, paper binders, another horse app, or something else?
3. What is the most frustrating records or paperwork problem you have had in the last year?
4. Where do sales slow down, become messy, or lose momentum because records are incomplete?
5. How often do you deal with ownership transfers, registration paperwork, or buyer packet prep?
6. What part of your current process is most manual or error-prone?
7. If one system organized your horse records and documents automatically, what would it absolutely need to do?
8. What would make you switch away from the setup you use today?
9. Who would use something like this every week, and who would approve payment?
10. If XBAR cleaned up records, tracked ownership integrity, and produced buyer-ready profiles, what would that be worth to your business?
11. Would you rather pay monthly software fees, a setup fee, or a combination?
12. Would you trust OCR-assisted document intake if there were a human review step before records became final?
13. If we offered record migration and setup for you, would that make adoption much easier?

## Follow-up Questions

Use these if the conversation is strong:

- How many horses are you actively managing today?
- How many documents or records would need to be migrated?
- How many people on your team need access?
- What kinds of buyers ask for documentation most often?
- What happens when ownership paperwork is delayed or incomplete?
- Have you ever lost time, trust, or money because records were messy?
- What is the most painful handoff between internal operations and a buyer or owner?

## What To Listen For

Strong signals:

- “We lose time chasing paperwork.”
- “Everything is spread across folders or emails.”
- “Sales get delayed because records are incomplete.”
- “Ownership details get messy.”
- “We need one clean packet for buyers.”
- “I would pay if this cleaned up the chaos.”

Weak signals:

- “Looks nice.”
- “Maybe someday.”
- “I already have a system and it works fine.”
- “This would be convenient.”

Best signal of willingness to pay:

- they describe a painful, repeated, expensive workflow
- they already spend time or money solving it badly
- they want migration or setup help
- they ask when they could use it

## Scoring Rubric

Score each interview from 1 to 5 on:

- paperwork pain
- ownership/transfer pain
- sales packet pain
- willingness to switch
- willingness to pay

Strong early target:

- total score 18+

## What We Are Trying To Prove

The product hypothesis is correct if interviews show:

- paperwork chaos is more painful than generic horse management
- ownership/transfer integrity is a meaningful business problem
- buyer-ready profiles and sale packets matter
- OCR-assisted intake sounds valuable
- the target customer will pay meaningful monthly fees plus onboarding

If interviews do not validate that, XBAR should narrow further before more feature expansion.
